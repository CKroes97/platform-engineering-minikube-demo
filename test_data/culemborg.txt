Short History (Highlights)

Early origins: First mention around 1281, tied to a castle built by the lord of Bosinchem. 
Geschiedenis Extra

City rights in 1318: Culemborg got formal city rights. 
Wikipedia
+1

Vrijstad (free city): For a long time, it was a fiefdom separate from what became the Netherlands; it had its own asylum and toll rights. 
Wikipedia

Castle and walls: A castle was built north of the city; in the 14th century, a city wall + moat were constructed. 
Geschiedenis Extra

Decline of independence: In the 18th century it lost much of its sovereign independence; later came under larger Dutch political structures. 
Wikipedia

Modern events:

In 1868, a railway bridge over the Lek was built — quite a feat at the time. 
Wikipedia
+1

1995: temporary evacuation due to flood risk. 
Wikipedia

Between 1994 and ~2009: development of ecological neighborhood Lanxmeer. 
Wikipedia

Notable Sights & Heritage

Binnenpoort / Lanxmeerpoort: A surviving medieval city gate — one of the most iconic landmarks. 
Exploring Holland

Orphanage (Elisabeth Weeshuis): Now a museum; gives insight into historical social care. 
Exploring Holland

Windmill “De Hoop”: A five-storey tower mill (stellingmolen), built in 1854, restored to working order — a Rijksmonument. 
Wikipedia

Castle Ruins / Historic Castle Site: The old Culemborg Castle (Kasteel Culemborg) remains only in ruins; now it’s a cultural park. 
Wikipedia

Fort Everdingen: Part of the New Dutch Waterline defense system; relevant for military history. 
Exploring Holland

Ecowijk Lanxmeer: A modern, sustainable neighborhood — a model of ecological urban planning. 
Exploring Holland
+1

Culemborg Railway Bridge: The tied-arch railway bridge over the Lek is historically important; originally built in 1868, rebuilt in 1983. 
Wikipedia

Demographics / Economy / Transport

Population trends: Growing; from ~27.6k in 2016 to ~29.1k in 2021. 
AllCharts.info

Economy: Historically trade and agriculture; modern Culemborg is residential with good links to bigger Dutch cities. 
nieuwstadinbeeld.nl

Transport:

Train: Culemborg has a station with direct lines to Utrecht and Den Bosch. 
Wikipedia

Bridge: The railway bridge over the Lek is a key connection. 
Wikipedia

Ferry: There’s a ferry across the Lek, connecting to the Betuwe region. 
Exploring Holland

Recent / Noteworthy Events

In 1995, Culemborg was evacuated due to a high risk of flooding. 
Wikipedia

Explosion: In 1991 there was a fireworks factory explosion, causing casualties. 
Wikipedia

Ecological urban planning: The Lanxmeer neighborhood development is considered quite innovative (sustainability, self-sufficiency). 
Wikipedia

Walk the historic centre: start around the Binnenpoort and explore old streets, markets, and monuments.

Visit De Hoop windmill on a Saturday (check local opening hours).
